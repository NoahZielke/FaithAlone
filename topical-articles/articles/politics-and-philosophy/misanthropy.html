Article Title

An increase in perceived agency is directly correlated to a lower sense of misanthropy. It thrives were "this sucks and there's nothing I can do about it" lives.

The cause of misanthropy is not nihilism. There are people who acknowledge that existence is objectively meaningless, yet do not hate humanity on that account. Likewise, misanthropy is not caused by other human beings "behaving badly" - acting foolish, polluting the earth, causing war and conflict, being shallow, committing crimes, and so on. Virtually all non-misanthropes recognize that humans behave badly.

Instead, it is caused by a combination of nihilism, fixating on the bad qualities of others, and intense dissatisfaction with one's own life - usually their appearance and social life - with the third factor being by far the most important one, which keeps one trapped in misanthropy.

Of course it is a matter of framing. Someone else in a misanthrope's exact same position socially may not be a misanthrope, because they have a much more healthy mindset, which keeps them from the kind of spiraling and catastrophizing which is required for misanthropy to take root. But, someone who is already misanthropic doesn't care about that yet. First, they need to be given a selfish motive

An increase in hope about one's own future is directly correlated to a decrease in misanthropy.

Misanthropy is romanticized in the mind of a misanthrope. "Look how stupid these people are. They think their lives have meaning. They delude themselves into thinking they matter. Humans are toxic. They are a blight on creation, and a cancer to earth. I get it. They don't. I see through the illusion of meaning." Etc.

A misanthrope, because they are intensely dissatisfied with their own life, reframes all of humanity in the most negative possible terms, as a defense mechanism.

Why does one bother to hate others?

John 6:47
