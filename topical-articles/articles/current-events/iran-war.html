Thoughts on the Iran War (2026)

In February 2026, regarding an earlier negotiation between Iran and US envoy Steve Witkoff aimed to resolve US grievances against Iran, including Iran's nuclear program, Witkoff stated:

Steve Witkoff - Interview (March 2, 2026)

Both the Iranian negotiators said to us, directly, with, you know, no shame, that they controlled 460 kilograms of 60% (enriched Uranium), and they're aware that that could make 11 nuclear bombs, and that was the beginning of their negotiating stance. (source)

Iran is ruled by a Shia Islamist government, and the government's worldview is essentially defined by being in opposition to America and Israel. After having been attacked as they have in this conflict - where their leader Ali Khamenei was killed, and their military is suffering extremely lopsided losses in comparison to the US and Israel - it is likely if the regime survives, they will do everything in their power to ensure that they can never be attacked in such a way ever again. If their nuclear weapon/ICBM program was underway before, it will likely be at the top or very near the top of the regime's priorities after this conflict. These would be tools to ensure that their regime survives, and is able to exert its will in the region, including continuing to fund proxy forces.

Consequently, early in the conflict, the US leadership stated that their goal was for regime change in Iran. The US president stated, "There will be no deal with Iran except UNCONDITIONAL SURRENDER!" (source). Earlier in the year, he had told Iranian protestors to "TAKE OVER YOUR INSTITUTIONS!!!" (source). However, after the leadership was assassinated, and the regime persisted, it became clear that it would be very difficult to actually dislodge the Iranian regime, and so the messaging on the goal of the conflict has evolved.

Now the question becomes whether there is a realistic off-ramp to the conflict without regime change. Likely, only if the US and Israel can convince the IRGC leadership to a nuclear and ICBM deal which pleases them. This would come about if the cost of continuing the war for the Iranian leadership becomes too high - whether because of casualties, internal unrest jeopardizing the regime's authority, or otherwise. The US wants an off-ramp to the conflict as soon as possible, because of the war's derailment of the global economy due to the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, and the unpopularity of long wars with the American public. So, despite Iran's unlikelihood of following through with any nuclear agreements, this conflict will probably see an end with a tenuous agreement - basically a return to the situation before the conflict.
