Moral Theology

Introduction

This article compiles the moral doctrine of the Bible. When examining the Hebrew Bible for moral law, the task of a moral theologian is to attempt to discern which commandments are intended as universal moral precepts, rather than commandments given to only certain individuals at a certain place and time, or laws given for the nation of Israel only as part of God's covenant with them, which while immoral when violated by a person under that covenant, are not intrinsically immoral under normal circumstances.

As a result, there will be some degree of controversy over certain laws, with different theologians holding different opinions as to which are binding upon all people today. Below is one attempt at gathering these moral laws. This is a very important study, because it creates a framework for a moral worldview that Christians maintain reflects the moral will of God for His creation, which is one of the most important subjects in religion.
