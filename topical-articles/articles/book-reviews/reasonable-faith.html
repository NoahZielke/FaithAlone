My Review of Reasonable Faith - By William Lane Craig

Introduction

Reasonable Faith is probably well-known Christian apologist William Lane Craig's best-known book. It is a reasoned, thoughtful argument in defense of what Craig formally calls "Christian Theism" (Introduction, pg. 24), that is, Christianity.

Review

Chapter 1 - How Do I Know Christianity Is True? gives a survey of historical approaches to arguing for Christianity. It includes a discussion on Craig's evidentialist approach to apologetics, and a brief discussion on valid and invalid argumentation. Among the topics discussed are forms of argumentation. For example, a deductive argument is one in which "the conclusion follows inevitably from the premises" (pg. 52). An inductive argument is one in which the truthfulness of the premises "render the conclusion plausible or likely, but do not guarantee its truth" (pg. 53).

Chapter 2 - The Absurdity of Life without God, does a great job at making the reader face the stark reality of the hopelessness and dismalness of being an accidental lifeform in a meaningless universe. This sets the stage for the rest of the book, which then pulls them out of the pit that they are dragged down into here.
