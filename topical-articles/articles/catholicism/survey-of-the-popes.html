A Survey of the Popes Disproves the Papacy

One of the most powerful proofs against the Papacy for those with eyes still open to see the truth can be seen in simply recognizing that:

  • 217 out of the 266 Popes have been Italian
  • Many of the Popes have been closely related to each other, for instance:
    • Pope Hormisdas was the father of Pope Silverius
    • Pope Felix III was the grandfather of Pope Gregory I
    • Pope Stephen II was the brother of Pope Paul I
    • Pope John XI was the uncle of Pope Benedict VII and Pope John XII, who himself was the half-uncle of the brothers Pope Benedict VIII and Pope John XIX, both of whom were uncles of Pope Benedict IX
    • Pope Gregory IX was the cousin of Pope Innocent III, and the uncle of Pope Alexander IV
    • Pope Innocent IV was the uncle of Pope Adrian V
    • Pope Honorius III was the granduncle of Pope Honorius IV
    • Pope Callixtus III was the uncle of Pope Alexander VI
    • Pope Clement VI was the uncle of Pope Gregory XI
    • Pope Gregory XII was the uncle of Pope Eugene IV
    • Pope Eugene IV was the uncle of Pope Paul II
    • Pope Sixtus IV was the uncle of Pope Julius II
    • Pope Pius II was the uncle of Pope Pius III
    • Pope Leo X was the granduncle of Pope Leo XI, and the cousin of Pope Clement VII
  • From the 11th-19th century, nearly 70 Popes appointed their relatives to be Cardinals, numbering around 200 - at least 15 of which later became Popes
  • Pope John XII was installed as Pope when he was 18 years old by his father Alberic II of Spoleto
  • Pope Benedict IX was installed as Pope when he was 20 years old by his father Alberic III of Tusculum
    • Pope Benedict IX then sold the Papacy to Pope Gregory VI. He later tried to depose him, and a dispute between the two, plus another Italian noble, Pope Sylvester III, had to be settled by the Council of Sutri (1046 AD)
